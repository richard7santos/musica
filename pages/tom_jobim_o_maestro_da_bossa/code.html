"Brazil's Gershwin"

"É pau, é pedra, é o fim do caminho..."

In 1972, Jobim composed Águas de Março (Waters of March). Often cited as one of the greatest songs ever written, its cyclical, stream-of-consciousness lyrics and descending melodic patterns mirrored the heavy tropical rains of Rio de Janeiro.

It was a testament to his ability to find the universal in the local—the philosophical in the mundane.
