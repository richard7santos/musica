Elis
Regina

Known as "Pimentinha" (Little Pepper), Elis was the definitive interpreter of the MPB movement, bridging the gap between the sophisticated bossa nova and the experimental Tropicália.

■ The Vocal Range

Technically impeccable, Elis possessed a mezzo-soprano range characterized by agile phrasing and a "metalized" timbre that could pierce through any orchestral arrangement.

PRECISION

Micro-Timing

Her ability to stay slightly behind or ahead of the beat was a signature bossa nova trait perfected into high art.

DYNAMICS

Control

From a whisper in Jobim's ear to a thunderous roar at the festivals, her dynamic range remains unmatched.

LIVE ON TV RECORD

O Fino da Bossa

The 1965 television show that cemented her as a superstar. Alongside Jair Rodrigues, Elis hosted a program that defined the aesthetic standards of Brazilian popular music for a generation.

  • Debuted "Arrastão" at the 1965 Festival.
  • The legendary partnership with Jair Rodrigues.

The album recorded at MGM Studios is often cited as the greatest Brazilian record ever made. It brought together Jobim's minimalism and Elis's technical exuberance.
